"Through unity, flight; through law, freedom."

The Council of Wings is the supreme legislative and representative body of the Democratic Republic of Pigeonaria. It exercises the will of the people through lawmaking, oversight, and public accountability. As defined by the Instrument of Government, the Council of Wings embodies the principle that all public power proceeds from the people and ensures that governance remains transparent, democratic, and lawful.

1. Role and Function

The Council of Wings serves as:

  • The national legislature, responsible for drafting, debating, and enacting laws.
  • The representative assembly, expressing the will of Pigeonaria's citizens.
  • The constitutional guardian, ensuring that all government actions comply with the law.
  • The check and balance on the executive branch, maintaining parliamentary oversight of the Prime Minister and the Ministries.

The Council's authority extends over every domain of public life—legislation, finance, defense, foreign relations, and the protection of civil liberties.

2. Composition and Membership

The Council of Wings initially consists of five members, expanding to eleven once the population surpasses the required threshold of registered citizens.

  • Each member holds an equal vote and equal standing in legislative procedure.
  • Members are elected through proportional representation once the national population reaches 31 citizens, ensuring every political movement or party receives fair representation based on vote share.
  • Until elections are held, the Council may operate with appointed interim members confirmed by citizen consensus.

Eligibility

Any registered citizen of the Republic, aged 18 or older, may stand for election to the Council, provided they are not under active disciplinary action, foreign allegiance, or conflict of interest.

3. Leadership Structure

  • Speaker of the Council – Presides over sessions, manages debates, ensures parliamentary order, and represents the Council in official functions.
  • Vice-Speaker – Assists and substitutes for the Speaker when required.
  • Prime Minister – Head of Government, elected by the Council from among its members, responsible for leading the executive branch and maintaining confidence in the legislature.
  • Deputy Prime Minister – Supports the Prime Minister in coordinating government policy, assumes duties in their absence, and ensures inter-ministerial cooperation.
  • Committee Chairs – Lead specialized committees (Legislation, Oversight, Foreign Affairs, etc.) and report their findings to the full Council.

The Speaker, Prime Minister, Deputy Prime Minister, and Vice-Speaker are elected by a majority of the Council's members during the first sitting after each general election.

4. Legislative Process

  1. Proposal Stage – Any Council member or the Prime Minister may introduce a bill. Citizens may also initiate proposals via the Citizen Initiative System if they gather sufficient support.
  2. Committee Review – The relevant standing committee examines the bill, assesses legality, and makes recommendations.
  3. Debate Stage – The full Council debates the bill in open session, allowing for public observation and record-keeping.
  4. Voting Stage – Laws pass by simple majority unless they amend the Constitution or affect neutrality, which require a supermajority (two-thirds).
  5. Ratification and Publication – Once passed, the law is signed by the Speaker and Prime Minister and published in the Government Gazette.

All legislative actions are publicly recorded to uphold transparency and citizen oversight.

5. Oversight Powers

The Council of Wings has the authority to:

  • Question and summon Ministers to explain policies, budgets, or actions.
  • Initiate Votes of No Confidence against the Prime Minister, Deputy Prime Minister, or individual Ministers.
  • Review all government expenditures through reports from the National Audit Office.
  • Investigate misconduct or corruption via collaboration with the High Commissioner for Government Oversight.
  • Approve or reject treaties, international agreements, and budgetary allocations.

These powers ensure that the executive branch remains accountable to the people through their representatives.

6. Committees

Standing committees operate under the authority of the Council to specialize in key areas:

  • Legislation Committee – Reviews all proposed laws for constitutional compliance.
  • Oversight Committee – Works with the High Commissioner to ensure transparency and legality.
  • Foreign Affairs Committee – Reviews treaties, international cooperation, and neutrality policy.
  • Finance and Treasury Committee – Examines budget proposals, Ducat management, and audit reports.
  • Defense and Security Committee – Monitors national defense policy, civil protection, and intelligence oversight.

Special committees may be formed for temporary matters such as constitutional reform, electoral review, or emergency inquiry.

7. Sessions and Procedures

  • The Council convenes ordinary sessions four times per term and extraordinary sessions as needed, called by either the Prime Minister, Deputy Prime Minister, or two Council members.
  • All sessions are public, recorded, and published in the Government Gazette, except in cases involving classified security matters.
  • A quorum of a simple majority (three-fifths of members) is required for votes to be valid.
  • Voting is conducted digitally and logged automatically to prevent tampering or irregularities.
  • Debates follow strict parliamentary etiquette; personal attacks, threats, or disruption result in censure or suspension.

8. Relationship with the Prime Minister and Government

  • The Prime Minister and Cabinet derive their legitimacy from the Council of Wings.
  • The Prime Minister must maintain confidence of a majority of members at all times.
  • The Deputy Prime Minister assists in day-to-day coordination and assumes responsibilities in the event of absence or incapacity of the Prime Minister.
  • A Vote of No Confidence can dissolve the government if passed by simple majority, triggering either the election of a new Prime Minister or, in some cases, a general election.
  • The Council reviews all executive decrees, budgets, and emergency actions.
  • The Prime Minister and Deputy Prime Minister may propose legislation but cannot enact it without Council approval.
  • The Council retains power to override ministerial actions found unconstitutional or unlawful.

This system ensures a balance between executive action and legislative oversight, preventing abuse of power.

9. Elections and Terms

  • General elections are triggered once the population threshold of 31 registered citizens is met.
  • Members serve four-year terms, after which a new election is called.
  • Elections are organized by the Independent Electoral Commission, ensuring impartiality and transparency.
  • Parties are registered with the Commission and must comply with campaign finance and ethics laws.
  • The first official Council election will be held once the population and administrative systems reach readiness in 2026–2027.

10. Constitutional Authority

Under the Instrument of Government, the Council of Wings is the only body empowered to pass legislation, approve budgets, and amend the Constitution. All other institutions—the Government, Judiciary, and Ministries—operate under its laws. The Council may:

  • Enact ordinary laws by majority vote.
  • Amend the Constitution with a supermajority and subsequent Council ratification.
  • Declare national emergencies (two-thirds vote required).
  • Approve or withdraw recognition of foreign states and treaties.
  • Authorize elections and referenda.

The Council's authority is supreme but bound by law, ensuring power remains both democratic and limited.

11. Transparency and Public Access

The Council operates under the Transparency Principle:

  • All minutes, voting records, and bills are publicly accessible via the Government Gazette.
  • Sessions are open for observation by citizens and foreign guests.
  • Non-public sessions are strictly limited to matters of national security or privacy.
  • Citizens may request transcripts, submit petitions, and propose legislation.
  • Every decision is documented to preserve accountability and historical accuracy.

12. Symbolism and Identity

The Council of Wings takes its name from the pigeon, the national symbol of peace, unity, and vigilance. Each member represents a "wing" of the Republic — distinct yet working in harmony to sustain flight.

"I pledge to uphold the Constitution, serve the Republic, and protect the people of Pigeonaria with honor and integrity."

The Council Chamber, located in Featherhold, serves as both a legislative hall and a symbol of civic equality. Its seal features three wings encircling the national emblem, representing law, order, and freedom.

13. Relationship with Citizens

Citizens are encouraged to engage directly with the Council through:

  • Petitions and Citizen Initiatives on national issues.
  • Referenda for major constitutional or diplomatic questions.
  • Observation of sessions, both digitally and in person.
  • Participation in committees or advisory panels on cultural, scientific, or local affairs.

The Council's legitimacy derives entirely from this active civic participation. No law or policy may be enacted without the foundation of public consent.

14. Interaction with Other Branches

  • With the Judiciary: The Council passes laws, but courts interpret them. The Constitutional Court can strike down Council acts that violate the Constitution.
  • With the Government (Executive): The Council grants or withdraws confidence in the Prime Minister and Deputy Prime Minister. The government must report on all major decisions.
  • With Oversight Bodies: The High Commissioner and Ombudsman report annually to the Council, ensuring ongoing accountability.
  • With Local Governments: Regional and municipal councils operate under national law but enjoy local autonomy as granted by the Council.

15. Modern Role (2026 and Onward)

As of 2026, the Council of Wings is in its foundational period, establishing parliamentary procedures, codifying electoral law, and preparing for the Republic's first democratic elections. Until a full electorate is achieved, interim members serve as stewards of the Constitution and custodians of national law.

The Council is the centerpiece of Pigeonaria's democratic system, ensuring that every future government remains subject to law, citizen consent, and legislative oversight.
