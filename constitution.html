THE INSTRUMENT OF GOVERNMENT

FUNDAMENTAL LAW OF THE DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC OF PIGEONARIA

ADOPTED BY THE CONSTITUENT ASSEMBLY

EFFECTIVE IMMEDIATELY

CHAPTER 1: FOUNDATIONS OF THE STATE

Art. 1. Sovereignty of the People

All public power in the Democratic Republic of Pigeonaria derives from the people. Sovereignty belongs unconditionally to the citizenry.

Art. 2. Principles of the Republic

The Republic is founded upon the principles of:

  • Free opinion and public debate
  • Universal and equal suffrage
  • Representative democracy through the Council of Wings

Art. 3. Supremacy of Law

All public power is exercised under the Law. No individual, office, or institution may act above statutes enacted by the Council of Wings.

Art. 4. Separation of Powers

Legislative, Executive, and Judicial powers are separate, independent, and interdependent. No branch may unlawfully interfere with another.

CHAPTER 2: PROTECTION OF DEMOCRACY

Art. 1. Parliamentary Confidence and Coalitions

The Government, led by the Prime Minister, must maintain the confidence of the Council of Wings.

Political parties may form coalitions to establish a majority.

A Vote of No Confidence may be initiated by any party or coalition. A simple majority vote against the Prime Minister dissolves the Government.

Art. 2. Constitutional Rigidity

Amendments to this Constitution require:

  • Approval by a majority of the current Council of Wings
  • Ratification by a subsequent Council following a general election
  • Public notice at least 60 days prior to the vote

Art. 3. High Commissioner for Government Oversight

The Council of Wings shall appoint a High Commissioner for Government Oversight, independent of political influence, with the authority to investigate any government agency or official. No document or communication shall be withheld under the "Transparency Principle."

The High Commissioner shall be appointed for a non-renewable six-year term by a two-thirds vote of the Council upon nomination by a multi-party selection panel. Removal shall require an independent judicial finding of misconduct or incapacity. The High Commissioner's budget shall be protected by law and may not be reduced during their mandate.

Art. 4. Anti-Corruption and Oversight

All officials must publicly disclose assets and potential conflicts of interest. Violation may result in removal from office and legal prosecution.

An independent Anti-Corruption Authority shall be established by law with investigative and prosecutorial referral powers, secured funding, and operational independence.

Art. 5. Protection of Political Pluralism

No single party may dominate the political system. Citizens may freely form political associations. Coalitions do not negate the rights of smaller parties to participate fully.

Art. 6. Citizen Safeguards

Citizens may petition the Council of Wings or initiate referenda on matters of public concern, ensuring direct oversight of major state decisions.

Art. 7. Independent Electoral Commission

An Independent Electoral Commission, appointed by a two-thirds vote of the Council of Wings on recommendation of a non-partisan selection panel, shall administer and supervise all national elections and referenda.

Members shall serve staggered six-year terms and may be removed only for cause by a three-quarters vote of the Council following a public hearing.

The Commission shall maintain and audit voter rolls, ensure transparent counting procedures, and publish results publicly.

Art. 8. Campaign Finance and Transparency

All campaign contributions and expenditures for national elections shall be publicly disclosed within seven days of receipt or payment.

Individual donations above a defined threshold established by law are prohibited.

Violation of campaign finance rules shall be punishable by criminal law and may result in disqualification of candidates or parties.

CHAPTER 3: FUNDAMENTAL RIGHTS AND FREEDOMS

Art. 1. Absolute Freedoms

The State shall never enact laws that infringe upon:

  • Freedom of Expression: Every citizen may criticize the State or public institutions without fear of reprisal
  • Freedom of the Press: Government censorship of media or publications is strictly prohibited
  • Property Rights: No private asset may be seized without due process and fair market compensation
  • Political Pluralism: Citizens may form political associations freely

Art. 2. Equality and Anti-Discrimination

All persons are equal under the law. Discrimination based on race, gender, religion, sexual orientation, or political affiliation is strictly prohibited.

Art. 3. Right to Privacy

Citizens have the right to private communication, digital security, and protection from unlawful surveillance.

Art. 4. Balancing of Rights

When fundamental rights come into conflict, the Judiciary shall apply the principle of proportionality and reasoned balancing, ensuring that the essence of no right is destroyed.

CHAPTER 4: THE COUNCIL OF WINGS AND THE PRIME MINISTER

Art. 1. Supreme Legislative Authority

The Council of Wings is the supreme legislative body of Pigeonaria. All laws originate here and are subject to review by the Judiciary for constitutionality.

Art. 2. Election of the Prime Minister

The Prime Minister is elected by the Council of Wings and must maintain majority support, which may include coalitions of multiple parties. Coalitions may form and dissolve as necessary, but the Prime Minister remains accountable to the Council.

Art. 3. Judicial Independence

Judges are independent, appointed for life, and may only be removed for cause through due process. The Judiciary ensures the Law remains separate from politics.

Judges of the Supreme and Constitutional Courts shall be nominated by a Judicial Selection Commission composed of legal experts and representatives from multiple parties, and confirmed by a two-thirds vote of the Council of Wings.

Removal shall occur only after an independent inquiry, public hearing, and two-thirds vote of the Council affirming cause such as corruption, incapacity, or gross misconduct.

Art. 4. Election Transparency

All elections, party nominations, and referenda shall be publicly verifiable. Ballot tampering or election interference is a high crime against the Republic.

Art. 5. Emergency Limitations

In times of national emergency, the Government may issue temporary measures. Such measures require Council approval within 30 days and may never infringe on Fundamental Rights or the independence of the Judiciary.

A national emergency may be declared only by the Council of Wings upon a two-thirds vote, limited to thirty days, and may be extended only by the same majority following judicial review confirming necessity and proportionality. All emergency decrees must be publicly reported and automatically expire if not renewed.

Art. 6. Legislative Terms

The term of the Council of Wings shall be four years, with general elections held within 60 days of the end of each term. The Council may be dissolved early only by a two-thirds vote or through a successful vote of no confidence that fails to produce a new government within 30 days.

CHAPTER 5: FOREIGN POLICY AND NEUTRALITY

Art. 1. Policy of Neutrality

The Democratic Republic of Pigeonaria shall remain neutral in all external military alliances. Full membership in any military or political alliance is prohibited.

Art. 2. Amendment of Neutrality

Any decision to enter a full alliance or alter the neutrality policy requires the affirmative vote of at least two-thirds of all members of the Council of Wings in a quorum session.

Art. 3. International Cooperation

Pigeonaria may engage in diplomatic, economic, or humanitarian cooperation, provided no such agreement compromises the State's neutrality or constitutional principles.

CHAPTER 6: AMENDMENTS AND REVIEW

Art. 1. Amendment Procedure

All proposals to amend the Constitution must follow the rules in Chapter 2, Article 2. Fundamental principles, including sovereignty, separation of powers, and neutrality, are protected by special supermajority requirements.

Art. 2. Constitutional Review

The Judiciary has the authority to review proposed amendments or laws to ensure compliance with this Constitution before enactment.

CHAPTER 7: THE GOVERNMENT AND THE MINISTRIES

Art. 1. The Government

The Government is composed of the Prime Minister and such Ministers as may be appointed. It exercises executive authority in accordance with this Constitution and the laws enacted by the Council of Wings.

Art. 2. Collegial Decision-Making

The Government shall deliberate collectively on all matters of general policy, proposed legislation, the national budget, and international agreements. The Prime Minister presides over meetings and ensures the unity of government policy.

Art. 3. Appointment of Ministers

The Prime Minister shall nominate Ministers, who shall be confirmed by a simple majority of the Council of Wings. Ministers may be dismissed by the Prime Minister or by a vote of no confidence in the Council.

Art. 4. Ministerial Responsibility

Each Minister is individually responsible for the management of their Ministry and collectively responsible for the decisions of the Government. Ministers are accountable to the Council of Wings and subject to questioning, interpellation, and inquiry.

Art. 5. Acting Government

If the Prime Minister resigns, dies, or is removed by a vote of no confidence, the Government shall act in a caretaker capacity until a new Prime Minister is elected by the Council of Wings.

Art. 6. Administrative Neutrality

Public administration shall be impartial, lawful, and efficient. Officials shall apply statutes faithfully without political bias. No Minister may issue directives in individual cases concerning the exercise of public authority except as provided by law.

Art. 7. Public Access to Government Activity

All citizens have the right to access official documents, decisions, and meeting minutes, unless restricted by law for reasons of national security or personal privacy. Openness is the rule; secrecy is the exception.

CHAPTER 8: LOCAL AND REGIONAL GOVERNMENT

Art. 1. Local Self-Government

The Republic shall be divided into regions and municipalities, each with elected councils possessing the right to manage local affairs under the law.

Art. 2. Autonomy and Oversight

Local authorities have the power to levy taxes, adopt local budgets, and issue regulations within their jurisdiction. National authorities may supervise legality but shall not interfere with local discretion in matters of local concern.

Art. 3. Cooperation and Solidarity

Regions and municipalities may form associations for shared services, economic development, and infrastructure, provided such cooperation respects constitutional principles and financial transparency.

Art. 4. Citizen Participation

Citizens may petition local councils, propose referenda on local issues, and attend council meetings, subject to orderly procedure.

CHAPTER 9: FINANCE AND PUBLIC ECONOMY

Art. 1. National Budget

The Government shall submit an annual budget to the Council of Wings no later than sixty days before the fiscal year begins. The Council shall adopt the budget by majority vote.

Art. 2. Fiscal Responsibility

All state revenues and expenditures must be authorized by law. Borrowing, guarantees, and state debt shall be limited to levels consistent with long-term financial stability.

Art. 3. Audit of Public Funds

An independent National Audit Office, answerable only to the Council of Wings, shall examine the accounts of the state, ministries, and public agencies. The results of audits shall be public and subject to parliamentary debate.

Art. 4. Public Procurement and Integrity

All public contracts shall be awarded through transparent and competitive processes. Corrupt or collusive practices are punishable under criminal law.

Art. 5. Equal Allocation of Resources

Public funds shall be distributed among regions and municipalities according to objective criteria, promoting equality of opportunity and balanced development across the Republic.

CHAPTER 10: CIVIL SERVICE AND THE OMBUDSMAN

Art. 1. Civil Service Principles

Employment in public administration shall be based on merit, competence, and integrity. Civil servants are accountable to law, not to political direction, in their official acts.

Art. 2. The Ombudsman of the Republic

The Council of Wings shall appoint an Ombudsman for a term of six years, independent of government or party control. The Ombudsman supervises the legality of government agencies and the protection of citizens' rights.

Art. 3. Powers of the Ombudsman

The Ombudsman may investigate complaints from citizens, initiate inquiries on their own motion, demand documents, summon officials, and refer cases for prosecution when law or rights are violated.

Art. 4. Reports and Accountability

The Ombudsman shall present an annual public report to the Council of Wings detailing findings, recommendations, and the implementation status of prior decisions.

CHAPTER 11: THE JUDICIARY AND COURTS OF LAW

Art. 1. Structure of the Courts

Judicial authority is exercised by the Supreme Court, the Constitutional Court, appellate courts, and such lower courts as the law may establish.

Art. 2. Constitutional Court

The Constitutional Court shall review the constitutionality of statutes, government decrees, and treaties. It may annul any act contrary to this Constitution.

Art. 3. Supreme Court

The Supreme Court is the highest authority in civil and criminal matters. Its judgments are final and binding on all authorities and individuals.

Art. 4. Appointment and Tenure

Judges of both the Supreme and Constitutional Courts are appointed in accordance with Chapter 4, Article 3. They serve until the age of seventy or voluntary retirement.

Art. 5. Judicial Review of Administration

Administrative courts shall ensure that public authorities act lawfully and respect citizens' rights. Citizens may challenge any administrative decision affecting them.

Art. 6. Transparency of Justice

Hearings shall be public, and judgments shall be reasoned and published, except where the law requires confidentiality for security or privacy.

CHAPTER 12: NATIONAL DEFENCE AND SECURITY

Art. 1. Civilian Control

All defence and security forces are subject to the authority of the Government and oversight by the Council of Wings. Military power shall never be used for political or partisan purposes.

Art. 2. Defensive Posture

The Republic maintains armed forces solely for defence of its territory, population, and constitutional order. Aggressive warfare is forbidden.

Art. 3. Emergency Mobilization

Any mobilization of armed forces beyond routine defence duties requires approval of the Council of Wings within seven days. Provisions in Chapter 4, Article 5, on emergency limitations apply.

Art. 4. National Security Council

A National Security Council, chaired by the Prime Minister and including representatives of the opposition and relevant ministers, shall coordinate defence and security policy and report annually to the Council of Wings.

Art. 5. Oversight and Transparency

All defence expenditures are subject to audit by the National Audit Office. Classified information shall be reviewed by a special parliamentary committee to ensure legality and proportionality.

CHAPTER 13: ENVIRONMENT AND FUTURE GENERATIONS

Art. 1. Right to a Sustainable Environment

Every person has the right to live in a healthy environment. The State and all public authorities shall safeguard natural resources for present and future generations.

Art. 2. Environmental Responsibility

Public and private actors shall prevent environmental harm, apply the precautionary principle, and restore damage to ecosystems at their own cost.

Art. 3. Climate and Biodiversity Council

An independent Climate and Biodiversity Council shall advise the Government and Council of Wings on long-term sustainability goals and monitor compliance with environmental law.

Art. 4. Public Participation

Citizens and organizations have the right to access environmental information, participate in decision-making, and seek judicial remedies against violations of environmental rights.

CHAPTER 14: FINAL PROVISIONS

Art. 1. Continuity of Law

All laws and regulations existing at the time of this Constitution's entry into force remain valid insofar as they do not conflict with its provisions.

Art. 2. Constitutional Oath

Before taking office, the Prime Minister, Ministers, and Members of the Council of Wings shall swear or affirm to uphold this Constitution and serve the people of the Democratic Republic of Pigeonaria with honesty and integrity.

Art. 3. Entry into Force

This Instrument of Government shall enter into force immediately upon adoption and shall serve as the supreme law of the land.

Art. 4. Interpretation

In the event of ambiguity, this Constitution shall be interpreted in favour of democracy, the rule of law, and the protection of human dignity.

CHAPTER 15: POLITICAL PARTIES AND ELECTIONS

Art. 1. Formation of Political Parties

Citizens may freely form political parties without undue restriction. Parties shall be registered with the Independent Electoral Commission and comply with transparency and democratic governance requirements.

Art. 2. Party Funding and Oversight

All political parties must maintain publicly disclosed financial records. The Electoral Commission may audit party finances and enforce penalties for violations.

Art. 3. Proportional Representation

Legislative elections shall aim to represent the diversity of political opinions proportionally. Minorities, women, and underrepresented groups shall be encouraged to participate to ensure inclusive governance.

Art. 4. Campaign Regulations

Campaigns shall be conducted fairly, without coercion or undue influence. Political advertising shall be subject to rules ensuring transparency, equity, and accuracy.

CHAPTER 16: CITIZEN PARTICIPATION AND REFERENDA

Art. 1. Right to Initiative

Citizens may initiate legislative proposals through petitions, provided signatures from a minimum threshold of the electorate are collected.

Art. 2. Referendum Procedure

Legally binding referenda may be held on major national issues. The Independent Electoral Commission shall supervise all referenda. Quorum and majority thresholds for approval shall be established by law.

Art. 3. Public Consultation

Before major legislation, the Government shall provide for public consultation periods, allowing citizens, experts, and civil society organizations to provide input.

CHAPTER 17: NATIONAL SYMBOLS AND IDENTITY

Art. 1. Flag and Emblems

The Republic shall have a national flag, emblem, and anthem as determined by law. These symbols represent the sovereignty, unity, and values of the nation.

Art. 2. National Holidays

The Council of Wings shall designate national holidays to commemorate significant historical, cultural, or civic events.

Art. 3. National Language

The official language of the Republic shall be English. The government shall ensure access to public services, legislation, and education in the official language while respecting linguistic minorities.

CHAPTER 18: CIVIL DEFENSE AND PUBLIC SAFETY

Art. 1. Civil Defense

The Republic shall maintain organized civil defense units to respond to natural disasters, emergencies, and humanitarian crises.

Art. 2. Public Safety Services

Police and emergency services operate under civilian oversight and shall maintain impartiality, professionalism, and respect for human rights.

Art. 3. Independent Oversight

A Civil Safety Oversight Board, appointed by the Council of Wings, shall monitor compliance with laws, investigate complaints, and recommend reforms to ensure public trust in safety institutions.

CHAPTER 19: NATIONAL PLANNING AND INFRASTRUCTURE

Art. 1. Strategic Planning

The Government shall prepare long-term national plans for infrastructure, transportation, energy, and technology development to ensure sustainable growth and public welfare.

Art. 2. Oversight of Public Projects

Major public works shall be subject to independent review for financial accountability, environmental compliance, and social impact.

Art. 3. Public-Private Cooperation

The Republic may enter partnerships with private entities to deliver public services, provided transparency, fairness, and public interest are safeguarded.

CHAPTER 20: CULTURE, EDUCATION, AND SCIENCE

Art. 1. Right to Education

Every citizen has the right to free and equal access to education at primary, secondary, and higher levels.

Art. 2. Academic Freedom

Educational and research institutions shall operate independently and promote critical thinking, scientific inquiry, and innovation.

Art. 3. Promotion of Arts and Culture

The Republic shall support arts, culture, literature, and national heritage. Citizens shall have the right to create, publish, and preserve cultural and scientific works.

Art. 4. Libraries, Museums, and Archives

The State shall ensure the preservation and accessibility of national archives, libraries, and museums for public education and cultural enrichment.

CHAPTER 21: HEALTHCARE AND PUBLIC WELFARE

Art. 1. Right to Healthcare

Every citizen has the right to accessible, affordable, and high-quality healthcare services. The State shall provide for preventive, curative, and emergency care.

Art. 2. Public Health Policy

The Government shall develop and implement public health policies to prevent disease, promote wellness, and ensure equitable access to services across all regions.

Art. 3. Social Welfare

The Republic shall provide social protection for those in need, including the elderly, disabled, unemployed, and vulnerable populations, consistent with the principle of equality.

Art. 4. Oversight of Health Services

An independent Public Health Oversight Board shall monitor service delivery, public spending, and compliance with healthcare standards, reporting annually to the Council of Wings.

CHAPTER 22: LABOR RIGHTS AND EMPLOYMENT

Art. 1. Right to Work

Every citizen has the right to work under fair, safe, and just conditions, without discrimination.

Art. 2. Collective Bargaining

Workers have the right to form unions, engage in collective bargaining, and participate in workplace decision-making.

Art. 3. Minimum Standards

The State shall establish minimum wages, occupational safety standards, and maximum working hours through legislation.

Art. 4. Protection Against Exploitation

Forced labor, child labor, and exploitative employment practices are strictly prohibited and punishable by law.

CHAPTER 23: ENVIRONMENT AND SUSTAINABILITY

Art. 1. Environmental Protection

The Republic shall ensure sustainable management of natural resources, prevent environmental degradation, and protect biodiversity.

Art. 2. Climate Action

The Government shall develop and implement strategies to mitigate climate change and reduce greenhouse gas emissions, in consultation with citizens, experts, and international standards.

Art. 3. Environmental Justice

Communities affected by environmental harm have the right to compensation and legal recourse.

Art. 4. Oversight Council

The Climate and Biodiversity Council (Chapter 13) shall monitor compliance, propose corrective actions, and advise the Government and Council of Wings on long-term environmental policies.

CHAPTER 24: NATIONAL DEFENSE, SECURITY, AND INTELLIGENCE

Art. 1. Civilian Control of Armed Forces

The armed forces are under civilian authority, accountable to the Government and the Council of Wings. Military personnel shall never interfere in political matters.

Art. 2. Defensive Doctrine

The Republic maintains armed forces strictly for defense of its territory, population, and democratic institutions. Offensive warfare is prohibited.

Art. 3. Intelligence Services

Intelligence agencies operate under strict legal authorization, judicial oversight, and respect for civil liberties. Surveillance and data collection require proportionality, necessity, and transparency where possible.

Art. 4. Parliamentary Oversight

A permanent Security and Defense Committee within the Council of Wings shall review military and intelligence activities, budgets, and strategic plans.

CHAPTER 25: INTERNATIONAL RELATIONS AND TREATIES

Art. 1. Sovereign Diplomacy

The Republic shall conduct foreign affairs to protect its interests, promote peace, and maintain neutrality, consistent with Chapter 5.

Art. 2. Treaty Ratification

All international treaties and agreements require approval by a two-thirds majority of the Council of Wings and review by the Constitutional Court for compatibility with the Constitution.

Art. 3. International Obligations

The Republic shall respect international law, human rights conventions, and environmental agreements, provided they do not conflict with fundamental constitutional principles.

Art. 4. Diplomatic Representation

The Government shall maintain embassies, consulates, and international missions to safeguard citizens abroad and foster cooperation with other nations.

CHAPTER 26: SCIENCE, TECHNOLOGY, AND INNOVATION

Art. 1. Scientific Advancement

The Republic shall promote research, innovation, and technological development in all fields to improve quality of life and economic competitiveness.

Art. 2. Open Access and Ethics

Research shall be conducted ethically, with open access to publicly funded studies, and must respect human rights, privacy, and environmental standards.

Art. 3. Innovation Councils

The Government shall establish independent advisory councils to guide policy on emerging technologies, digital infrastructure, cybersecurity, and artificial intelligence.

CHAPTER 27: NATIONAL EMERGENCY PREPAREDNESS

Art. 1. Definition of Emergency

A national emergency may include natural disasters, epidemics, severe economic crises, or threats to constitutional order.

Art. 2. Declaration and Oversight

Emergency powers may be invoked only under the procedures established in Chapter 4, Article 5. The Council of Wings and Constitutional Court shall supervise all emergency actions.

Art. 3. Limits and Duration

Emergency measures must be proportional, temporary, publicly reported, and automatically reviewed within thirty days. Renewal requires approval by two-thirds of the Council of Wings and judicial review.

CHAPTER 28: DIGITAL GOVERNANCE AND CYBERSECURITY

Art. 1. Digital Rights

Every citizen has the right to access digital services, online information, and public communications. Freedom of expression, privacy, and data protection apply equally in digital environments.

Art. 2. Cybersecurity

The State shall maintain secure digital infrastructure for public administration, elections, and critical services. Cyber attacks against the Republic or its citizens are crimes and subject to strict penalties.

Art. 3. Open Government Data

Government datasets, unless restricted for security or privacy reasons, shall be publicly accessible, allowing citizens, researchers, and civil society to monitor and improve public services.

Art. 4. Digital Oversight

The Independent Electoral Commission, Ombudsman, and relevant audit authorities shall have oversight of digital systems used for governance, elections, and public services.

Art. 5. Technology Ethics

Artificial intelligence, automated decision-making, and digital platforms used in governance shall comply with ethical standards, transparency requirements, and protections for human rights.

CHAPTER 29: TRANSPARENCY, ANTI-CORRUPTION, AND ACCOUNTABILITY

Art. 1. Principle of Transparency

All government actions, decisions, and financial management shall be conducted transparently, unless legally restricted for security, privacy, or national interest.

Art. 2. Strengthening Anti-Corruption Measures

The Independent Anti-Corruption Authority (Chapter 2, Art. 4) shall have authority to investigate, prosecute, and recommend reforms to prevent corruption at all levels of government.

Art. 3. Whistleblower Protections

Individuals reporting misconduct, corruption, or breaches of law within public or quasi-public institutions shall be protected from retaliation, including dismissal, harassment, or legal sanctions.

Art. 4. Public Reporting

The Government, Ombudsman, Audit Office, and Anti-Corruption Authority shall submit annual public reports on institutional performance, compliance, and investigations.

Art. 5. Citizen Oversight

Citizens may request public hearings, submit petitions, and access information to hold officials accountable. Government must respond within a reasonable timeframe under law.

CHAPTER 30: TRANSITIONAL AND ENFORCEMENT PROVISIONS

Art. 1. Continuity of Government

All laws, regulations, and administrative measures in force at the time of this Constitution's entry into effect remain valid insofar as they do not conflict with its provisions.

Art. 2. Transitional Institutions

All offices, councils, commissions, and agencies established under prior law shall be reviewed, confirmed, or reappointed under this Constitution within six months of its entry into force.

Art. 3. Enforcement of the Constitution

The Constitutional Court and Supreme Court shall ensure compliance with this Constitution. Any violation by individuals, officials, or institutions shall be subject to judicial remedies, including annulment of unlawful acts, removal from office, and sanctions.

Art. 4. Oath of Office

All public officials, including the Prime Minister, Ministers, Members of the Council of Wings, judges, and oversight authorities, shall swear to uphold this Constitution and serve the Republic faithfully.

Art. 5. Supremacy of the Constitution

This Constitution is the supreme law of the land. No law, decree, treaty, or administrative act may contravene its provisions. All government authorities, citizens, and institutions are bound by it.

Art. 6. Amendment and Future Safeguards

Amendments shall follow the procedures in Chapter 6. Fundamental principles of democracy, separation of powers, citizen rights, neutrality, and oversight institutions are protected by supermajority requirements and cannot be abolished.
