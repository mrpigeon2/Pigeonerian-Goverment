The Constitutional Court is the guardian of the Instrument of Government, ensuring all laws, executive actions, and treaties comply with the Constitution. It defends the democratic order, neutrality, and rule of law that form the foundation of the Republic.

Role & Powers

  • Reviews laws, decrees, and treaties for constitutional compliance.
  • Protects citizens' fundamental rights under the Constitution.
  • Can strike down or suspend unconstitutional acts.
  • May conduct preventive review of proposed laws before enactment.
  • Oversees constitutional matters during national emergencies.

Current Status

The Constitutional Court has been formally established under Chapter 11 of the Constitution. Judicial appointments are awaiting the creation of the Judicial Selection Commission.

Until the Court convenes, constitutional interpretation is handled provisionally by the Council of Wings through special legal review committees.

"Defending the Constitution, Upholding the Republic."

The Supreme Court is the highest authority in civil, criminal, and administrative justice. It ensures equal application of law, protection of individual rights, and accountability of public power.

Role & Powers

  • Final court of appeal for all lower courts.
  • Interprets and applies national laws in accordance with the Constitution.
  • Resolves civil, criminal, and administrative disputes.
  • Upholds due process, equality before the law, and fair trial standards.

Composition

Judges:
0 (to be appointed)
Appointing Authority:
Judicial Selection Commission (pending) + Council of Wings (2/3 confirmation)
Divisions:
Civil, Criminal, Administrative (planned)
Location:
Featherhold National Justice Complex

Current Status

The Supreme Court has been constitutionally established but remains unstaffed pending the formation of the Judicial Selection Commission.

During this interim period, unresolved legal questions are handled by the Council of Wings' Legal Affairs Committee, whose decisions are recorded in the Government Gazette.

"Equal Before the Law."
